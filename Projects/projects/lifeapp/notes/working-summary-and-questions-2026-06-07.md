# LifeApp Working Summary And Questions - 2026-06-07

## Working Thesis

LifeApp is for motivated fitness and health users who already track workouts, wearables, food, sleep, and habits, but are tired of spreading their day across Garmin, Whoop, Wahoo, Strava, calorie trackers, and habit apps.

The core promise is one clear daily health operating system: train better, recover better, eat better, and build better habits without needing six separate apps.

## Initial Product Shape

- Unified dashboard for health, training, sleep, recovery, food, water, habits, and progress.
- Wearable/device compatibility, starting from high-value integrations such as Garmin, Whoop, Wahoo, and later broader sources.
- Food logging through photo recognition, barcode/QR scanning, database search, and manual fallback.
- Macro, micronutrient, water, and daily-goal tracking.
- Sleep and recovery page that makes wearable data easier to understand and act on.
- Habit-building section that teaches users how to build habits, not just tick boxes.
- Strava-style social layer for sharing progress, motivation, and accountability.
- Later AI trainer layer with coach personas for running, lifting, cycling, and structured plans.

## Suggested MVP Wedge

The sharpest v1 wedge looks like:

1. Wearable recovery/training dashboard
2. Easy food logging
3. Habit-building system

That gives LifeApp a clear reason to exist before it becomes a full super app. The risk with launching everything at once is that the product becomes wide but shallow.

## Ten Moments The App Must Nail

1. Onboarding and goal setup
2. Connecting a wearable/device
3. Daily dashboard check-in
4. Food photo logging
5. Barcode/manual food logging
6. Water tracking
7. Habit check-in and reminders
8. Recovery/sleep insight
9. Weekly progress summary
10. Social share or accountability moment

## Questions For Charley, Vance, And Team

1. Who is the first target user: gym lifters, runners, cyclists, hybrid athletes, or general health improvers?
2. Which integration matters first: Garmin, Whoop, Wahoo, Strava, Apple Health, or Google Fit?
3. Is food logging the main hook, or is the unified health dashboard the main hook?
4. Should the first version be iOS-only, Android-only, or both?
5. Should social be public-feed style like Strava, private groups, friend comparisons, or just share cards?
6. What should be free, and what should sit behind premium?
7. How aggressive should ads be in the free version?
8. Does the app need coaching in v1, or should coaching wait until the core tracking loop works?
9. What does "AI personal trainer" mean first: chat advice, weekly plan generation, form checks, or habit nudges?
10. What is the one metric that proves v1 is working: daily active use, food logs, workouts synced, habits completed, or paid conversions?

## Things Not To Build First

- Full AI trainer marketplace.
- Too many device integrations at launch.
- Complex public social network.
- Advanced meal planning.
- Medical-grade health recommendations.
- Deep custom workout programming before the tracking loop is proven.
